Why You Should Choose WordPress

In this article, we’re going to discover exactly what WordPress is, why it’s awesome and why it sucks. WordPress is the most popular CMS on Earth but what is a CMS? A Content Management System (CMS), is a way for website owners, editors, and authors to quickly and effectively manage the content of a website. Users don’t need to edit code and can instead edit text and media from directly within their web browser or from a mobile application. This is very valuable for non-techies as it essential means that they can edit their website using a word processor like interface.

What is WordPress?

WordPress is a CMS that originally began as a blog platform; it has since evolved into something much more though. Today WordPress accounts for more than 25% of all Internet traffic, it also accounts for more than half of all websites running a known CMS. It’s not just little blogs using WordPress either, it’s also used by large established brands such Ted, Tech Crunch, CNN, Time Magazine, and the New York Post.

Advantages of WordPress

Simple to Use

One of the biggest advantages of WordPress is its ease of use, there’s nothing to really learn. You just sign in and start typing, once you’re happy with what you’ve got hit the publish button and WordPress does the rest. Simple.

Open Source

WordPress is open source meaning that web developers and designers are free to completely edit all themes and plugins to their hearts content. Due to this fact, WordPress has a massive community of experienced users and developers behind it. Exactly want you want when using building a brand.

Plugins

At the time of writing WordPress has 40,000+ known plugins, there are also many more which are used as custom plugins and functions. There are plugins for just about everything, want to make your WP site more secure?, there’s a plugin for that. Want to make your site load quicker?, there’s a plugin for that too. Wanna back up your site?, yep there’s a plugin!

Themes

Themes are a way to change the look and feel of a WordPress installation, there are many reputable sources that provide fantastic themes. The official WordPress website is always a good place to start, then there’s ThemeForest, Themify, Template Monster, and if you really want to stand out from the crowd you can always get a custom designed site.

Disadvantages of WordPress

It’s Popular

WordPress is everywhere and because it’s everywhere, it becomes a juicy target to hackers and spammers. If hackers can exploit a WordPress hole they can literally attack millions of websites.

Plugins

Plugins can be both a positive and a negative, for all the power and awesomeness that they bring they can also be a giant pain the neck. Large blogs such as CSS Tricks can use anywhere from 40 to 50 plugins and managing that many plugins can be really tricky.

When to use WordPress

As you can see WordPress literally is as a platform, then on top themes and plugins can be built to enhance the experience. So WordPress can be built into just about any type of website. But just because it can, doesn’t mean it should. If you’re building a site that doesn’t need to be updated in a regular fashion, then it’s a good idea to stray away from CMS’s altogether and instead utilize a static approach.

Frameworks such as Anchor CMS are another approach for smaller sites that aren’t looking to serve hundreds of posts or pages and instead are looking for a lightweight solution. Larger sites and those massive sites (YouTube, Facebook, Twitter i.e.) are built using more custom solutions, so while WordPress is a good idea for the majority of the Internet it’s not the perfect solution for every site.

So in summary:

– WordPress is a versatile platform
– Currently home to over 25% of Internet traffic
– Plugins and themes can enhance your websites
– Not ideal for really really simple sites or complicated sites
